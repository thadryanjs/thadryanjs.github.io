The peanut-butter and jelly of paired data: zip() and dict()

Paired data in Python is user-friendly and efficient, and I find myself using it all the time. Sometimes in the course of a workflow, we end up with with multiple simple lists of information that would be easier managed as paired data. If you find yourself in this situation, there are two simple tools that work in a really intuitive way to ‚Äúzip‚Äù the lists together such that the first item of list A is paired with the first item of list B.
